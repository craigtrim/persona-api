# Big Five Personality Archetypes

Estimated **20 core archetypes** that cover ~80% of recognizable personality patterns.

## Why 20?

**Mathematically**: 3,125 combinations (5^5 domain scores), or 759,375 counting all facet permutations.

**Practically**: Personalities become recognizable when traits are notably high (4-5) or low (1-2). A score of 3 is essentially "not a defining feature." Users relate to ~4-6 defining traits, not 15 facets.

## Domain Key

| Code | Domain | Low Trait | High Trait |
|------|--------|-----------|------------|
| A | Agreeableness | Cold, skeptical | Warm, trusting |
| C | Conscientiousness | Chaotic, flexible | Focused, organized |
| E | Extraversion | Reserved, solitary | Energetic, social |
| N | Negative Emotionality | Calm, stable | Anxious, reactive |
| O | Open-Mindedness | Conventional, practical | Creative, curious |

---

## Notes

- Many trait combinations are psychologically rare or unstable in practice
- Some combos are nearly synonymous (e.g., High A + Low N both suggest agreeability under stress)
- These archetypes are starting points, not rigid categories
- Facet-level variation creates meaningful subtypes within each archetype
